House rules on this board

Moving pieces
  • Pawns move forward one square, or two from their starting row if both squares are clear. They capture diagonally.
  • Knights jump in an L shape. Bishops use diagonals, rooks use files and ranks, and queens can do either.
  • Kings move one square. Castling is allowed when neither piece has moved, the path is clear, and the king does not pass through check.
  • The board blocks moves that leave your own king in check.
Promotion and en passant
  • When a pawn reaches the far side, choose a queen, rook, bishop, or knight.
  • En passant is available only on the very next move after an opposing pawn rushes two squares beside your pawn.
Winning
  • Check, checkmate, and stalemate are detected. If nobody has a legal move, the message will tell you what happened.
